The Earth Surface Mineral Dust Source Investigation (EMIT) mission from NASA is not specifically designed to study greenhouse gases, but its data can be used to learn more about how dust interacts with greenhouse gases in the atmosphere.

EMIT's data is being used to study how dust affects the Earth's climate in a number of ways. For example, scientists are using EMIT's data to:

  • Study how dust affects the formation and properties of clouds.
  • Study how dust affects the absorption and reflection of sunlight by the Earth's atmosphere.
  • Study how dust interacts with other greenhouse gases, such as water vapor and carbon dioxide.
